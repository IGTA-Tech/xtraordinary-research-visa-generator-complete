## 4. Competing Businesses

## 5. Confidentiality

During and after the Engagement Period, the Beneficiary shall not divulge or appropriate to his own use or to the use of others, in competition with Petitioner, any secret, confidence, or knowledge pertaining to Petitioner's business, obtained by Beneficiary in any way while being represented by the Petitioner as his U.S. agent/Employer.

## 6. Remedies

If at any time Beneficiary materially violates any of the covenants or agreements in this Agreement, Petitioner shall have the right to terminate all of its obligations to make further payments under this Agreement. The Beneficiary acknowledges that Petitioner would be irreparably harmed by a violation of this Agreement and agrees that Petitioner shall be entitled to an injunction restraining the Beneficiary from any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement, or to any equitable remedy without bond or other security being required.

## 7. Amendment and Termination

(a) This Agreement may be amended or canceled by mutual agreement of the parties without the consent of any other person and, so long as the Beneficiary lives, no person, other than the parties hereto, shall have any rights under or interest in this Agreement or the subject matter hereof.

(b) The Engagement Period shall terminate the earliest of:
1. Termination of the Beneficiary's right to be employed in the United States,
2. Termination of the Engagement, with or without cause, and with or without notice, by either the Beneficiary or Petitioner. The parties acknowledge and agree that the Engagement relationship set forth herein is an "Engagement-at-will" relationship.

## 8. Notices

Any notice required or permitted to be given under this Agreement shall be effective only if delivered by registered mail to Petitioner at its principal place of business or to Beneficiary at the last known address filed by Beneficiary in writing with Petitioner.


## 9. Non-Assignment

The interests of the Beneficiary under this Agreement are not subject to the claims or creditors and may not be voluntarily or involuntarily assigned or alienated.
